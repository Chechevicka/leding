Dear CSR Director,

I am writing to you on behalf of the Ukrainian NGO "Kosmos Tabir" to propose a circular economy partnership that addresses both the EU solar waste challenge and a critical humanitarian crisis.

Every year, thousands of functioning solar panels are decommissioned across Europe. Recycling them immediately is energy-intensive and expensive. At the same time, frontline Ukrainian communities have been completely cut off from the electrical grid due to direct shelling, leaving local medical outposts, administrative hubs, and schools in total blackout.

We install decentralized solar power systems on critical public infrastructure — village councils, local clinics (FAPs), schools, and emergency resilience hubs. 

**Our Proposal:**
Instead of storing or recycling functioning decommissioned PV modules, we request to redirect modules with 80%+ remaining efficiency to our humanitarian installations in Ukraine.
* We manage customs clearance, safety QA, and distribution to public facilities.
* We seek partners to assist with shipping and logistics from EU warehouses.
* We provide full verification reports (with photos, GPS coordinates, and recipient signatures) for every panel deployed.

For a frontline clinic or aid center, a panel at 80% efficiency is not waste—it is the difference between local emergency healthcare and complete isolation. 

We are supported by the Ministry of Energy of Ukraine. I have attached our project presentation and roadmap to this email. Would you be open to a quick call next week to discuss potential logistics?
