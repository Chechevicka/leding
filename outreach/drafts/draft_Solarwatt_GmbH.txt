My name is Nazar Botvynko, and I lead humanitarian innovations at NGO "Kosmos Tabir" (Kyiv, Ukraine). I am writing to you because of Solarwatt GmbH's leadership in the European solar sector and your commitment to sustainability.

As you may know, the ongoing war has completely destroyed the electrical grid in hundreds of Ukrainian frontline villages. In regions like Kherson, entire communities survive without electricity. Centralized grids cannot be rebuilt under active shelling, leaving local administrative offices, medical outposts (FAPs), and kindergartens completely in the dark.

To address this, we install decentralized solar stations on key public community buildings — enabling doctors to treat patients, local authorities to coordinate aid, and residents to charge emergency communication tools at local resilience hubs ("Points of Invincibility").

**How we can cooperate:**
Europe is facing a growing solar panel decommissioning challenge. We want to redirect retired, surplus, or cosmetically worn solar panels from your inventory (minimum 80% remaining efficiency, 200W+ capacity) to power this critical community infrastructure in Ukraine. 

* We coordinate custom waivers and distribution directly to public buildings.
* We seek logistics partners to assist with transport from EU warehouses to Ukraine.
* Your donation supports circular economy practices, provides an immediate lifeline to frontline communities, and yields a documented CSR story.

To date, our organization has successfully electrified 40+ public administrative buildings and deployed over 400 micro-solar systems. Our operations are officially coordinated with the Ministry of Energy of Ukraine and regional administrations.

I have attached our brief presentation and roadmap to this email. Could we schedule a brief 10-minute call next week to discuss how we might set up a collection of retired panels?
