## 1. Solar Manufacturers, Installers & Distributors
* **Subject Focus**: ESG, Circular Economy, panel donation (80%+ efficiency) for public admin buildings, medical outposts, and kindergartens.
* **Target Audience**: Corporate sustainability officers, directors of marketing, ESG leads.

```text
Subject: Partnering to Power Frontline Ukrainian Communities: Decommissioned PV Modules for Public Infrastructure

Dear {contact_name},

I hope this email finds you well. 

My name is Nazar Botvynko, and I lead humanitarian innovations at NGO "Kosmos Tabir" (Kyiv, Ukraine). I am writing to you because of {company_name}'s leadership in the European solar sector and your commitment to sustainability.

As you may know, the ongoing war has completely destroyed the electrical grid in hundreds of Ukrainian frontline villages. In regions like Kherson, entire communities survive without electricity. Centralized grids cannot be rebuilt under active shelling, leaving local administrative offices, medical outposts (FAPs), and kindergartens completely in the dark.

To address this, we install decentralized solar stations on key public community buildings — enabling doctors to treat patients, local authorities to coordinate aid, and residents to charge emergency communication tools at local resilience hubs ("Points of Invincibility").

**How we can cooperate:**
Europe is facing a growing solar panel decommissioning challenge. We want to redirect retired, surplus, or cosmetically worn solar panels from your inventory (minimum 80% remaining efficiency, 200W+ capacity) to power this critical community infrastructure in Ukraine. 

* We coordinate custom waivers and distribution directly to public buildings.
* We seek logistics partners to assist with transport from EU warehouses to Ukraine.
* Your donation supports circular economy practices, provides an immediate lifeline to frontline communities, and yields a documented CSR story.

To date, our organization has successfully electrified 40+ public administrative buildings and deployed over 400 micro-solar systems. Our operations are officially coordinated with the Ministry of Energy of Ukraine and regional administrations.

I have attached our brief presentation and roadmap to this email. Could we schedule a brief 10-minute call next week to discuss how we might set up a collection of retired panels?

Thank you for your time and solidarity.

Warm regards,

Nazar Botvynko
Head of Humanitarian Innovation Solutions
NGO "Kosmos Tabir"
Kyiv, Ukraine
```

## 2. Solar Recycling & Waste Management Associations
* **Subject Focus**: Environmental impact, circular economy, redirecting 80%+ panels to public infrastructure.
* **Target Audience**: Waste managers, circular economy leads, recycling compliance coordinators.

```text
Subject: Circular Economy for Ukraine: Repurposing Retired PV Modules for Frontline Public Infrastructure

Dear {contact_name},

I am writing to you on behalf of the Ukrainian NGO "Kosmos Tabir" to propose a circular economy partnership that addresses both the EU solar waste challenge and a critical humanitarian crisis.

Every year, thousands of functioning solar panels are decommissioned across Europe. Recycling them immediately is energy-intensive and expensive. At the same time, frontline Ukrainian communities have been completely cut off from the electrical grid due to direct shelling, leaving local medical outposts, administrative hubs, and schools in total blackout.

We install decentralized solar power systems on critical public infrastructure — village councils, local clinics (FAPs), schools, and emergency resilience hubs. 

**Our Proposal:**
Instead of storing or recycling functioning decommissioned PV modules, we request to redirect modules with 80%+ remaining efficiency to our humanitarian installations in Ukraine.
* We manage customs clearance, safety QA, and distribution to public facilities.
* We seek partners to assist with shipping and logistics from EU warehouses.
* We provide full verification reports (with photos, GPS coordinates, and recipient signatures) for every panel deployed.

For a frontline clinic or aid center, a panel at 80% efficiency is not waste—it is the difference between local emergency healthcare and complete isolation. 

We are supported by the Ministry of Energy of Ukraine. I have attached our project presentation and roadmap to this email. Would you be open to a quick call next week to discuss potential logistics?

Sincerely,

Nazar Botvynko
Head of Humanitarian Innovation Solutions
NGO "Kosmos Tabir"
```

```text
**How {company_name} can help:**

We do not transport batteries (we source/install standard new components locally), so the cargo consists strictly of inert solar panels, inverters, cables, and mounting structures.
```

```text
Subject: Sponsoring Decentralized Solar Electrification for Frontline Ukrainian Public Infrastructure

Dear {contact_name},

On behalf of NGO "Kosmos Tabir," I am writing to present a high-impact, immediate-relief project that restores energy security to community infrastructure in active combat and de-occupied zones in Ukraine.

We resolve this by installing off-grid solar stations on key public buildings. We pair funding with donated, decommissioned solar panels (80%+ efficiency) from the EU. This model is exceptionally cost-effective:

* **$10,000** covers the complete solar electrification of one frontline settlement — village council, medical outpost, and local kindergarten, establishing a reliable local energy grid.
* Sponsoring a region establishes decentralized energy security for local clinics and emergency resilience points serving thousands of citizens.

We have already successfully electrified 40+ public buildings and deployed 400+ micro-solar installations. Our operations are officially coordinated with the Ministry of Energy of Ukraine.
```
