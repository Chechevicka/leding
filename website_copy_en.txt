=== 3. THE PROBLEM SECTION ===
[problem_title]
    475 Ukrainian settlements have no electricity. Some for three years.

[problem_desc_1]
    Sustained attacks on power infrastructure have left entire frontline communities permanently cut off from the grid. There is no power to charge a phone, run a medical device, or keep a router alive. Families cannot call emergency services. The elderly cannot signal for help. Children do their homework by candlelight — if they have candles.

[problem_desc_2]
    The grid cannot be rebuilt under active shelling — centralized infrastructure is a target, and every repair gets destroyed again. The only viable path is decentralized: off-grid, home by home, built to survive.

[problem_quote]
    “In the Kherson region, over 26,000 households have had no electricity for almost three years.”

[problem_source]
    Ministry of Energy of Ukraine, July 2024


=== 4. OUR SOLUTION SECTION ===
[solution_title]
    A $10 solar converter — engineered for survival

[solution_desc]
    We design and produce a rugged, ultra-affordable voltage converter that works directly from any solar panel — no heavy batteries required. It takes raw, unstable solar input and delivers clean, stable USB power to charge phones, satellite routers, walkie-talkies, and medical equipment.

[sol_feat_1_title]
    Works with any panel

[sol_feat_1_desc]
    Wide 18V–60V input range — functions reliably with cracked, used, aged, or shrapnel-damaged panels. If it generates voltage, we can use it.

[sol_feat_2_title]
    4 simultaneous USB outputs

[sol_feat_2_desc]
    3×5V for smartphones and routers, plus 1×9-12V auto-detect for power banks, walkie-talkies, and tools — the whole household charges at once.

[sol_feat_3_title]
    10m weatherproof outdoor cable

[sol_feat_3_desc]
    Standard MC4 connectors — the panel mounts safely on the roof or wall outside, while the converter and all devices stay protected indoors.

[sol_feat_4_title]
    No training required

[sol_feat_4_desc]
    Designed to be used by elderly people and children with zero technical knowledge. Plug-and-play, tested stable in active combat zones.


=== 5. PROOF SECTION (FIELD-TESTED UNDER FIRE) ===
[proof_badge]
    Verified Field Case

[proof_title]
    Even a shrapnel-hit panel keeps working

[proof_desc]
    Our decentralized approach means there is no single point of failure. A damaged panel loses surface area but keeps generating current. When the Beryslav installation survived a nearby bomb strike with zero downtime, it confirmed what we already knew: small, distributed, off-grid solar is more resilient than any centralized power station in a war zone.

[proof_caption]
    Two weeks after installation in Beryslav district, a guided bomb detonated nearby. The panel took multiple shrapnel hits — the converter inside kept running without interruption, charging phones and communication tools throughout.

=== 6. EU RECYCLING & SECOND LIFE SECTION ===
[euro_title]
    Your decommissioned solar panels are a lifeline here

[euro_desc_1]
    Every year, millions of solar panels are taken out of service across Europe — slightly worn, cosmetically cracked, or simply replaced by newer models. Recycling them is expensive. Many sit in warehouses for years or get shipped overseas as waste.

[euro_desc_2]
    For a family in a frontline village with no grid, a panel at 80% efficiency isn’t “old technology” — it’s the only source of power they’ll have. We accept panels from 200W with 80%+ remaining efficiency. Cracked is fine — up to 10-20% of a batch. We can coordinate customs clearance, but we seek logistics partners to help with transport.

[euro_reframe_title]
    No storage costs. No recycling fees. Real humanitarian impact.

[euro_reframe_desc]
    Donating your decommissioned panels to Kosmos Tabir means they power real homes instead of sitting in a warehouse. You get a documented humanitarian use case — we get the panels to the families who need them most.

=== 9. ABOUT / ORIGIN STORY ===
[about_quote]
    “We started as a creative community. War turned us into humanitarian engineers.”

[about_desc]
    In 2020, we organized cultural rail expeditions to the Kherson region — the GogolTrain project, connecting artists and communities across Ukraine. When the full-scale invasion devastated those exact places, we didn’t walk away. We redirected everything into building solar infrastructure for the communities we knew personally. That is how Kosmos Tabir became a humanitarian organization.
